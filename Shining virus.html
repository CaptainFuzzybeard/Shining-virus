Overview

The Shiny Virus (Scientifically: Luminodermus pseudomorbus) is a newly identified member of the coronavirus family that specifically targets keratinocytes in the human epidermis. Unlike its predecessors, SHD-19 binds to CD147 receptors in skin cells, causing rapid hyperkeratinization and disruption of natural skin exfoliation processes.

Key Scientific Facts

  • Zoonotic origin: Believed to have jumped from Pangolins to humans
  • R0 of 3.8 (Highly Contagious)
  • Survives on surfaces for up to 72 hours
  • Triple-membrane structure makes it resistant to alcohol-based sanitizers
